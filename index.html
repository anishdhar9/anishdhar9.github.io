Supersonic Flows in Sod Shock Tube
PythonEuler EquationsHyperbolic PDE

Numerical solution of the 1D Euler equations for the classical Sod shock tube problem. Implements two explicit finite-difference schemes — Lax and MacCormack — and benchmarks their accuracy and stability across varying grid subdivisions and CFL numbers. Captures the three characteristic wave structures: rarefaction fan, contact discontinuity, and shock front.

Governing eqs.
1D Euler (conserved form: rho, rho*u, E)
Schemes
Lax (1st-order) vs. MacCormack (2nd-order predictor-corrector)
Parameters swept
Grid subdivisions N, CFL number (stability analysis)
Output
rho, u, p profiles vs. exact Riemann solution
